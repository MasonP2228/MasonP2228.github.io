tARget

In one of my classes during the spring of 2025, I was tasked with making an AR experience. I decided to create a simple game using Unreal Engine. From Unreal, I packaged my project for Android, and people in the class could play it on a Galaxy tablet.
In the game, users have 30 seconds to hit as many targets as they can. Targets appear randomly within a range of motion around their device's initial position. Users can tilt and move their device to aim, and they can tap the screen to fire a ball at a target.
This was my first time working with a game engine, and I learned a lot about Unreal from this project. In particular, I learned how to use Unreal's node-based approach to programming.
